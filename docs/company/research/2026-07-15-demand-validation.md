## 1. MeetMagic — how the category leader actually works

- **Founder: Carl Gough** (ex-IT sales, Sydney) — not Callum Laing as one internal note guessed.
  ([balancethegrind.co interview](https://balancethegrind.co/interviews/carl-gough-founder-ceo-of-meetmagic/))
- **Unit economics:** vendor pays ~**$1,000/meeting** for a curated 45-min "pitch-free" discovery
  meeting; **~70% (~$700) donated** to the exec's chosen charity; free for execs; vendors also pay an
  annual subscription per their Q&A PDF. Application-gated both sides; "limited numbers accepted each
  month." ([meetmagic.org](https://www.meetmagic.org/) ·
  [businesses.com.au](https://www.businesses.com.au/reports/latest-business-news/421935-new-world-first-b2b-platform-lets-c-level-executives-turn-time-into-money-for-charities))
  Package example from the founder: **"$5,000 for five meetings."**
  ([Jeff Bullas podcast](https://www.jeffbullas.com/podcasts/27-new-selling-method/))
  *(Corrects the 07-12 log's "~US$700/meeting" — $700 is the donated share of a ~$1,000 fee.)*
- **Supply recruitment (the operational core):** founder phone calls + LinkedIn + referrals, routed
  through **one charity partner** — Starlight Children's Foundation, whose board members at
  Macquarie/PwC/Deloitte opened CSR doors to executives. Founder's personal ask accept rate:
  **"for every 10 that I'd asked, it'd be 3 or 4 that would say yes."** First transaction was fully
  concierge (personally asked the Kathmandu CIO to take a $500 meeting). Repeat usage exists: some
  execs "six, seven, eight, nine, 10 meetings… 'Fill my diary up.'" *(all self-reported, same podcast)*
- **Traction reality check:** ~4 years to the first ~$40k donated
  ([businesses.com.au](https://www.businesses.com.au/reports/latest-business-news/421935-new-world-first-b2b-platform-lets-c-level-executives-turn-time-into-money-for-charities));
  COVID-era relaunch grew 60 execs/$20k → 1,000 execs/$1M in ~2 years
  ([iTnews case study](https://www.itnews.com.au/video/case-study-how-meetmagic-turns-a-simple-meeting-into-a-charitable-act-600603));
  today $1.4M / 2,900+ connections / 3,500+ decision-makers *(site, self-reported)*. That is
  **<1 meeting per registered exec over ~9 years** — registered supply massively exceeds transacted
  demand even at the leader — and ~0.6% of the founder's stated "$250M by 2026" goal. Bootstrapped,
  self-described profitable. Churn/annual volume: not found publicly.
- **Demand proof points:** early client Longtail UX turned its first meeting into a **$100k+ contract**
  *(self-reported)*; Commonwealth Superannuation Corp's CTO endorses the channel (iTnews above).

## 2. Gated postmortem (nearest demand-side analog)

- **Correction: Gated shut down Sept 30, 2023** (not 2024 as logged 07-12), ~2.5 years after a $3.5M
  seed ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/gated-launches-donation-based-email-management-solution---raises-3-5m-seed-round-301507928.html)).
  Official reason: **"our market isn't as big as we expected."** Lifetime: ~500M emails filtered,
  **~$500k to charity**. ([goldpenguin.org](https://goldpenguin.org/blog/gated-bids-farewell/))
- **What it proves / doesn't:** Gated failed on the **receiver (supply) side** — too few people
  willing to install an inbox gate — not demonstrably on sender willingness-to-donate (senders
  generated the $500k in ~$2 micro-donations). It validates that cold outreachers will pay small
  donations for access, while showing the protected-person side is the scarce one. **That maps
  exactly onto DonaTalk's observed constraint (2 real bookable listeners).** No deeper founder
  postmortem found publicly.

## 4. Cold-start playbook (two-sided B2B meeting/expert marketplaces)

1. **Supply-first is the default** (14/17 marketplaces in [Lenny's study](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/how-to-kickstart-and-scale-a-marketplace-9ee)),
   but DonaTalk currently fails **both** liquidity tests — underutilized supply AND near-zero demand
   ([supply-vs-demand-constrained test](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/how-to-know-if-youre-supply-or-demand)).
2. **B2B reversal — pre-sell demand with LOIs, then hand-build supply** (Andrew Chen,
   [Stripe Atlas guide](https://stripe.com/guides/atlas/andrew-chen-marketplaces)): get signed/paid
   commitments from 5–10 vendors *before* recruiting more listeners.
3. **Atomic network: tiny and specific** ([Chen](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/atomic-network) ·
   [cdixon single-player mode](https://cdixon.org/2015/01/31/come-for-the-tool-stay-for-the-network/)).
   MeetMagic's atomic network = one charity + its corporate boards + founder calls at 30–40% accept.
4. **Concierge, founder-operated:** [Intro.co ran 500 curated sessions in 2 beta months with the
   founder calling every customer](https://techcrunch.com/2021/10/13/intros-app-attracts-andreessen-seven-seven-six-kevin-durant-to-connect-celebrity-e-design-experts-with-those-seeking-advice/);
   MeetMagic still manually schedules every meeting. Nothing in the literature supports leaving a
   self-serve platform as the growth vehicle at zero liquidity.
5. **Charity-as-channel:** partner with ONE charity and use its board/CSR relationships to recruit
   exec supply and confer legitimacy — MeetMagic's distinctive, cheapest-replicable move.

## Evidence summary for the Board

- **Strongest FOR demand:** the only direct comp self-reports $1.4M donated across 2,900+
  connections at ~$1,000/meeting vendor pricing, with named blue-chip execs repeating 6–10 meetings
  and a documented $100k contract from a first meeting; charity-for-access separately proven at the
  high end ($53M Buffett/Glide, $610k Cook coffee).
- **Strongest AGAINST:** the model is thin and slow even for the winner — ~4 years to its first
  ~$40k, ~0.6% of its stated goal, <1 meeting per registered exec, zero funded imitators found; the
  nearest analog (Gated, $3.5M raised) died in 2.5 years on "our market isn't as big as we expected."
- **The binding constraint is buyable, not SEO-able:** MeetMagic solved listener supply with founder
  phone calls (30–40% accept) through one charity's board network — not product, ads, or search.
  DonaTalk's 2-listener problem is a founder-outreach problem — consistent with the 07-14 audit.
- **Canonical playbook fit:** pre-sell demand (LOIs/prepaid packages) → hand-recruit a tiny atomic
  supply network → run 100% concierge.
- **Minimal viable test (founder-time cost only, ~4–6 weeks):** 1 charity partner + ~30 personal
  executive asks (expect ~10 accepts at MeetMagic's rate) + 3 prepaid vendor packages (~$5k/5
  meetings each) in one vertical, fully manual. If vendors won't prepay ~$15k against a
  MeetMagic-priced offer with real named listeners, the external evidence says reallocation is
  justified.
