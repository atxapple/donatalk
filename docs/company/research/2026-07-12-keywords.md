## 2. Adjacent-category scan: pay-for-access / paid-meeting marketplaces (first-ever pass)

Prior competitor scans only searched donation-outreach phrasing. This pass mapped the nearest adjacent
category — who charges money for access to people — and it corrects the record.

### 2a. MeetMagic — direct competitor, previously missed
- **Model = DonaTalk's mechanic:** vendors pay for a 45-min "discovery meeting" with a C-level executive;
  ~US$700/meeting with **~70% donated to the exec's chosen charity**; free for executives. Founder Carl Gough
  (Australia). Site updated June 2026; claims *(self-reported)* 3,500+ members across 5 countries and US$1.4M
  donated; enterprise testimonials (Ogilvy UK/SG/NZ/AU). Calls itself "world first."
  [meetmagic.org](https://www.meetmagic.org/) · [96five](https://96five.com/finance-and-business/new-company-turns-meeting-fatigue-into-charitable-donations/) ·
  [businesses.com.au](https://www.businesses.com.au/reports/latest-business-news/421935-new-world-first-b2b-platform-lets-c-level-executives-turn-time-into-money-for-charities) ·
  [iTnews case study](https://www.itnews.com.au/video/case-study-how-meetmagic-turns-a-simple-meeting-into-a-charitable-act-600603)
- **Surviving differentiators for DonaTalk:** MeetMagic is **curated/enterprise, exec-side-vetted,
  "pitch-free"-framed, APAC/UK-weighted, fixed-price (~$700, ~70% pass-through)**. DonaTalk remains the
  **self-serve, any-vertical, everyday-seller, Listener-priced (4.9% fee)** product. The wedge holds; the
  "only players are Influence Board/TGN/HearMeOut" claim does not.
- **SEO reality:** MeetMagic ranks on its brand + "executive meetings" phrasing, **not** on "donation for a
  meeting"-family terms — the content/search surface for the category is still unclaimed.
- **§6 check (done today):** our live explainer already avoids "first/only" claims and frames DonaTalk by
  differentiators — nothing published is contradicted. Future content: stay MeetMagic-aware; never claim
  "first/only."

### 2b. Gated — the cautionary shutdown
[Gated](https://techcrunch.com/2022/03/24/too-much-email-try-gated-which-asks-unknown-senders-to-make-a-donation-first/)
(unknown senders donate to reach your inbox, 70% to nonprofit; min $2/email) **shut down Sept 2024**: "our
market isn't as big as we expected" after filtering ~0.5B emails and raising ~$500K for charity
([goldpenguin.org](https://goldpenguin.org/blog/gated-bids-farewell/)). Differences that matter: Gated sold
*inbox filtering* (passive, low-value unit, consumer-priced); DonaTalk sells a *booked meeting* (active,
high-value unit). Still — it is the strongest counter-evidence on donation-gated-attention demand and belongs
in the risk register: **category demand must be manufactured, which is exactly what the Cluster C strategy says.**

### 2c. The rest of the category (none charity, none seller-side)
- **Charity-auction access:** [Charitybuzz](https://www.charitybuzz.com/) dominates (auction lots incl.
  ["pitch meetings"](https://www.charitybuzz.com/catalog_items/auction-45-minute-pitch-meeting-with-media-303204)
  — but Hollywood/VC one-offs, consumer-framed, ~15–20% buyer's premium, no B2B category). Buffett lunch
  legacy rebooted **May 2026** as "A Seat at the Table" (Buffett + Currys, eBay/GLIDE)
  ([Forbes 2026-05-13](https://www.forbes.com/sites/alexanderpuutio/2026/05/13/from-power-lunch-to-power-table-buffett-and-the-currys-reboot-iconic-auction/)) —
  fresh press keeps "pay for access, money to charity" culturally legible; a PR/content hook, not a keyword.
  [CharityStars](https://www.charitystars.com/) alive (celebrity meet-and-greet); Omaze exited experiences;
  IfOnly unverified/inactive.
- **Expert-call marketplaces:** [Intro.co](https://intro.co/marketplace), [Minnect](https://www.minnect.com/),
  [Clarity.fm](https://clarity.fm/expert), [MentorPass](https://www.mentorpass.co/),
  [Office Hours](https://officehours.com/), [Topmate](https://topmate.io/features/meetings) — all alive, all
  buyer-pays-expert, **zero charity models, zero B2B-sales positioning** across a
  [10-platform 2026 roundup](https://talkspresso.com/blog/best-platforms-paid-1on1-expert-calls-2026).
- **Enterprise expert networks** (GLG/AlphaSights): compliance frameworks **explicitly forbid** using calls to
  sell ([glg.com/compliance](https://glg.com/compliance)) → positioning line: *sellers have no sanctioned paid
  path to prospects — except one that funds the prospect's cause.*
- **Warm-intro infra:** [Commsor "go-to-network"](https://www.commsor.com/warm-intros) ($1k–6.5k/mo,
  team-network activation) and [Boardy.ai](https://www.boardy.ai/) (AI superconnector, 114K+ intros claimed,
  Boardy Pro $100/mo June 2026) — both alive and content-flooding warm-intro vocabulary; neither manufactures
  intros where no relationship exists; no donation mechanic. Still cite-and-partner, not rivals.
- **"Prospect gets paid for the meeting" (no charity): no live named player found.** "Pay per appointment/
  meeting" SERPs are all seller-pays-*agency* lead-gen ([saleshive](https://saleshive.com/glossary/pay-per-appointment-lead-generation),
  [belkins](https://belkins.io/blog/pay-per-appointment-lead-generation)) — the opposite payment direction.
  Avoid building on that phrase family (mis-categorization risk). Nearest analogs: ABM charity-donation gifting
  ([Sendoso](https://www.sendoso.com/resources/blog/charity-donations-transform-account-based-marketing-plays));
  **Loop & Tie** lets gift recipients donate the gift's value — closest existing gifting-world analog
  ([teamsunday roundup](https://teamsunday.com/blog/top-corporate-gifting-platforms-2026)).

### 2d. Vocabulary map (what has SERP activity, who owns it)
- Saturated / avoid: "pay per appointment/meeting" (lead-gen agencies, wrong payment direction); "charity
  auction" family (consumer/celebrity, Charitybuzz); "warm intro" head terms (Commsor/Boardy flooding).
- **Unowned (our whitespace):** "donation for a meeting" · "get paid to take sales meetings" (prospect side)
  · "buy someone's time" — near-zero product-owned SERPs. Seed the prospect-side family: *"get paid in
  donations to take sales meetings" / "your time funds your cause"* — nobody owns it, and it targets the
  **Listener-acquisition** side no prior pass addressed.
