### 3a. Who owns the SERPs
Appointment-setting/SDR-outsourcing agencies own every head term — Belkins, SalesHive, Cleverly,
Salesar, RevNew, Only-B2B, Leads at Scale, DemandNexus, danishleadco — all writing bottom-of-funnel

[cleverly.co](https://www.cleverly.co/blog/cost-per-sales-meeting) ·
[belkins.io](https://belkins.io/blog/appointment-setting-costs-pricing-models) ·
[saleshive.com glossary](https://saleshive.com/glossary/cost-per-meeting/) ·
[demandnexus.io](https://www.demandnexus.io/appointment-setting-costs/)). **Zero big-brand SaaS, zero
independent research firms.** Winning format: long-form pricing guide + comparison table + FAQ + year
in title, refreshed annually.

### 3b. The primary-source vacuum (the strategic finding)
- The most-recycled figure — "**according to Clutch**, avg qualified B2B appointment = $550–$1,700" —
  circulates across vendor blogs ([priceithere.com](https://priceithere.com/appointment-setting-services-cost/)
  et al.) but **no primary Clutch survey page could be located** (Clutch's own page is just a
  [directory](https://clutch.co/call-centers/appointment-setting)). Treat as unverified.
- Vendors demonstrably cite each other: danishleadco's [2026 CPM roundup](https://danishleadco.io/blog/what-is-the-cost-per-meeting-for-outbound-in-2026)
  aggregates Cleverly/Leads-at-Scale/OSP/IntentAmplify numbers.
- Benchmark pages become citation magnets: [FirstPageSage's CPL/CPSQL reports](https://firstpagesage.com/reports/average-cost-per-lead-by-industry/)
  are cited by sopro.io, martal.ca, cleverzebo.com; SPOTIO's stats page propagates via SugarCRM,
  bookyourdata.com, wavecnct.com.
- **Conclusion: a methodology-transparent "What a B2B sales meeting really costs (2026)" page — showing
  its arithmetic (fully-loaded SDR ÷ held meetings; conference package ÷ meetings; agency PPM menus,
  each sourced) — would be the only primary-source node in a self-citing vendor graph.** The r/sales
  **$15k/17 meetings ≈ $880/meeting** conference comp (quote bank, run 39) fits this genre exactly.

### 3d. Demand evidence (Google suggest, 2026-07-14)
- **Real today:** "pay per meeting" → suggests *lead generation / agency / service / booked / ppm /
  meaning* — richest B2B set in the family. "appointment setting cost" → *services cost / b2b / **ai
  appointment setter cost*** (emerging). "cost per sql" → *meaning / benchmark* (but SERP collides with
  MS SQL Server licensing).
- **Latent:** "cost per sales meeting" / "cost per booked meeting" → zero suggestions, yet 8–10 agencies
  maintain fresh 2025/2026 pages on them (they can see tool volume we can't).
- **Term collisions to avoid in titles:** "cost per meeting" (internal-meeting-cost intent — Koalendar/
  MeetingKing/MeetGeek own it) · "cost per appointment" (healthcare) · "appointment setting" bare
  (job-seeker intent: *is appointment setting legit / a good side hustle*).
- **Calculator surface:** no ungated buyer-side sales-CPM calculator exists — [outboundsalespro's](https://outboundsalespro.com/cost-per-meeting/)
  is email-gated; HubSpot's CPL templates gated; Search Engine Land has only a simple [CPL widget](https://searchengineland.com/tools/ppc-ad-calculators/cpl-calculator).
  DonaTalk `/calculator` is already live and ungated — it needs the *vocabulary* (page copy/metadata
  currently frames cold-outreach cost, not "cost per booked meeting") and a benchmarks companion.
- **Question queries:** "should you pay for sales meetings" / "is it worth paying for appointment
  setting" → weak, mostly-vendor SERPs with no neutral decision content — cheap FAQ/PAA coverage on the
  stats page, not standalone posts. Reddit/forum indexed threads on these: null this pass (don't
  overweight the single $880 comp as ongoing forum discourse).
