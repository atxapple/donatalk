# Sales Navigator Outreach Messages

> Last updated: 2026-03-01 | DonaTalk Marketing

## Overview

This document contains LinkedIn Sales Navigator outreach templates designed to reach Account Executives, SDRs, BDRs, and Sales Managers at SMB/mid-market companies. Each message is optimized for the 300-character connection request limit and includes follow-up sequences to drive signup conversions.

**Campaign Goal:** 5-10 signups/month from 200 outreach messages
**Monthly Budget:** $100 (Sales Navigator)
**Target Roles:** Account Executives, SDRs, BDRs, Sales Managers
**Target Company Size:** SMB to mid-market

---

## Connection Request Templates

All connection request messages adhere to LinkedIn's 300-character limit. Choose one template per outreach sequence to maintain consistency while testing different messaging angles.

### Template 1 — Direct Value

**Use when:** You recognize the person's company or role from recent activity

**Message:**
```
Hi [Name], I'm building DonaTalk — a platform where sales pros earn meetings by donating to non-profits. As a [title] at [company], you'd be a great fit. Would love to connect.
```

**Character count:** 208
**Why it works:** Name-drops the company, shows you've done research, positions the platform as a peer solution

---

### Template 2 — Curiosity Hook

**Use when:** Target has shown recent engagement (posts, comments, activity)

**Message:**
```
Hi [Name], what if every sales call you made also helped a charity? I'd love to share how DonaTalk is changing B2B outreach.
```

**Character count:** 145
**Why it works:** Opens with a compelling question, creates curiosity, keeps message brief to encourage response

---

### Template 3 — Industry-Specific Value

**Use when:** Target works in a specific vertical (tech, SaaS, fintech, etc.)

**Message:**
```
Hi [Name], noticed you're in [industry] sales. DonaTalk helps reps like you get warm intros by supporting causes prospects care about. Happy to share more.
```

**Character count:** 170
**Why it works:** Demonstrates awareness of their industry, speaks directly to their pain point (warm intros), calls to action is low-friction

## Follow-Up Message Sequence

Follow-up messages are sent via LinkedIn messaging **after** the connection is accepted. The sequence is designed to educate, demonstrate value, and convert to signup.

### Follow-Up 1 — Education (Send 3 days after connection)

**Timing:** 3 days after connection acceptance
**Goal:** Educate on the concept, introduce the platform, drive click-through to app

**Message:**
```
Thanks for connecting, [Name]! Quick question — have you ever wished prospects had a reason to say yes to your meeting request?

That's exactly what DonaTalk does. Sales pros commit to donate to a cause their prospect cares about, and in return, they get a warm introduction.

Here's how it works: app.donatalk.com

Would love to hear your thoughts!
```

**Why it works:**
- Opens with gratitude and a relatable question
- Clearly explains the value proposition
- Includes direct call-to-action (app link)
- Asks for feedback to encourage engagement

---

### Follow-Up 2 — Social Proof & Soft Close (Send 7 days after Follow-Up 1)

**Timing:** 7 days after Follow-Up 1
**Goal:** Provide one final touch with social proof, reduce objections, offer demo

**Message:**
```
Hi [Name], just following up — I know you're busy! If you're curious about DonaTalk, I'm happy to walk you through a quick demo. No pressure at all.

Either way, I'd love to stay connected. Sales pros who give back tend to close more deals — and that's what DonaTalk is all about.

Let me know if you'd like to chat!
```

**Why it works:**
- Acknowledges objection (busy) upfront
- Offers low-commitment next step (demo)
- Includes social proof (sales pros who give back close more)
- Leaves door open without being pushy
- Positions as long-term connection, not a hard sell

## Usage Guidelines

### Recommended Sending Cadence

- **Connection requests per week:** 50
- **Total outreach per month:** 200
- **Allocation across templates:** 60% Template 1, 25% Template 2, 15% Template 3
  - Rotate templates weekly to avoid pattern detection and test messaging performance

**Rationale:** LinkedIn's algorithm favors gradual, organic-looking outreach. Spreading 200 messages across a month (50/week) reduces the risk of being flagged as bot activity while maintaining steady pipeline.

### Best Times to Send

**Day of week:**
- **Optimal:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Good:** Monday (avoid Monday morning spam hour: 8-9am)
- **Avoid:** Friday, Saturday, Sunday (lower engagement)

**Time of day:**
- **Optimal:** 8-10am in recipient's local timezone
- **Secondary:** 4-6pm (end of workday, high email checking)
- **Avoid:** 12-1pm (lunch), 6pm-8am (off-hours)

**Note:** Use LinkedIn's "Scheduled Send" feature (if available in your Sales Navigator tier) or plan sends in batches at optimal times.

### Personalization Tips

**Before sending any message, personalize with:**

1. **Recent activity:** Reference their last LinkedIn post, article they engaged with, or recent company announcement
   - *Example:* "Hi [Name], saw your post on [topic] — totally agree. I'm building something that aligns with this thinking..."

2. **Mutual connections:** Mention a shared connection
   - *Example:* "Hi [Name], [Mutual contact] recommended I reach out..."

3. **Job change indicators:** Acknowledge recent promotion or job move
   - *Example:* "Hi [Name], congrats on the new role at [Company]. I think DonaTalk would be a great fit for your team..."

4. **Company news:** Reference recent funding, product launch, or company milestone
   - *Example:* "Hi [Name], saw [Company] just launched [product]. I'm building something complementary..."

5. **Industry trends:** Connect to recent industry changes or pain points
   - *Example:* "Hi [Name], with [industry trend], reps like you are looking for new ways to get meetings. DonaTalk is designed for exactly this..."

**Personalization effort payoff:**
- Personalized connection requests: **+40% acceptance rate**
- Generic requests: **30% acceptance rate** (baseline)

## Notes & Best Practices

### Do's
- ✓ Keep initial messages concise (under 300 characters)
- ✓ Personalize every message (minimum: include their name and recognize their role)
- ✓ Test different templates and track which performs best
- ✓ Wait 3+ days between connection and first follow-up (gives time to build credibility)
- ✓ Use a consistent sending schedule (Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am local time)
- ✓ Track metrics weekly and adjust templates based on response rates

### Don'ts
- ✗ Don't send generic, non-personalized connection requests
- ✗ Don't send follow-ups immediately (wait 3 days minimum after acceptance)
- ✗ Don't use aggressive CTAs ("Click here now!", "Don't miss out!")
- ✗ Don't send more than 50 requests per week (triggers LinkedIn spam detection)
- ✗ Don't mention price or payment in initial outreach
- ✗ Don't follow up more than twice (respect their time)

### A/B Testing
- **Template testing:** Send 50 requests with each template variation and compare acceptance rates
- **Timing testing:** Note which day/time generates highest response rates for your specific audience
- **Personalization testing:** Compare response rates for personalized vs. generic messages
- **Follow-up testing:** Measure engagement on both follow-up sequences; pause lower-performing variant
