Everyone I know who runs a team gets the same inbox: dozens of vendor pitches a week, a few of which might genuinely be worth 30 minutes. There's no honest signal for telling which ones.

I'm building DonaTalk to be that signal — and this is the listener side of it:

You pick a nonprofit you care about and set a donation amount (from $10). A seller who wants your time commits that donation to your cause along with their pitch. You accept the ones worth hearing. If you decline, nothing is charged to anyone.

You never pocket a cent — the donation goes to the nonprofit you chose. Your calendar quietly becomes a fundraising channel for a cause you actually care about.

New page on how it works: https://app.donatalk.com/for-listeners

Honest question for the operators here: is there a donation size that would make you take a cold vendor meeting?
